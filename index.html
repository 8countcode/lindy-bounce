How far forward you ride. It rolls the heel up, shifts your centre of mass forward, and shrinks the base of support to the forefoot — and the contact point keeps moving through the bounce (heel settling at the bottom, ball at push-off).

Dynamics & stance

One pulse per beat. Faster tempo → a sharper, bigger impact spike for the same bounce size.
How gradually you absorb the bottom reversal. Soft (high) spreads the impulse over time → low spike; stiff (low) = a hard, injurious slam.
The bendy foot as a shock absorber: rolling onto the ball lets the arch + toe/intrinsic flexors spring and soak up the bounce, sparing the knees — but it loads the forefoot, plantar fascia & Achilles.
Lindy shifts weight foot-to-foot. 50% = shared across both feet; 100% = full single-leg, which roughly doubles this leg's load.
